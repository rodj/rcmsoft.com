About RCMSoft

The person behind the company

RCMSoft is me — Robert Martin, a software developer based in the Atlanta metro area.

I've been writing software professionally for over 20 years, working across a range of industries including finance, real estate, and e-commerce. I've built everything from internal line-of-business applications to customer-facing web apps to automation pipelines that quietly eliminate hundreds of hours of manual work per year.

Why I started RCMSoft

A lot of small and mid-size businesses in Atlanta have real software problems they're solving with spreadsheets, copy-paste, or manual effort — not because they don't know better, but because getting a development shop involved feels expensive and opaque. You don't always know who's actually doing the work, what it's going to cost, or whether the person you're talking to has any idea what they're talking about technically.

I wanted to offer something different: a direct relationship with a senior developer who will give you a straight answer about what's worth building, what isn't, and how to get there without overengineering it.

What I work with

My primary stack is C# / .NET and Python for back-end and automation work, with SQL Server for data. On the web side I work with standard HTML/CSS/JavaScript and have done significant work with Shopify's theme and app APIs. I also use AI tools heavily in my own workflow — including building custom integrations with the Claude API and MCP servers — so I can offer genuinely modern solutions, not yesterday's patterns.

Note on working with me When you hire RCMSoft, you're working with me directly. I don't subcontract your project or hand it to someone junior after we've agreed on scope. That means the person you talk to is the person who writes your code.

Outside the office

I'm a lifelong Atlanta resident who runs, reads, and spends too much time thinking about software architecture. I care about the Atlanta business community and prefer working with local clients I can meet in person when it makes sense.

Get in touch

The best way to start is to send me a message describing what you're trying to accomplish. I'll read it, think about it, and give you a real response — not a sales call request.
